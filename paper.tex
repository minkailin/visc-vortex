\begin{abstract}
  Vortex formation through the so-called Rossby wave 
  instability (RWI) in protoplanetary discs has been invoked to play a
  role in planet formation theory, and has been suggested to explain
  the observation of large dust asymmetries in transitional discs.   
%  However, previous studies of the RWI in 3D discs have shown that the
%  instability is global in the direction perpendicular to the disc
%  midplane. Thus physical conditions away from the disc midplane
%  may thus affect large-scale vortex formation through the RWI. 
  However, whether or not vortex-formation operates in layered
  accretion discs, i.e. protoplanetary discs including dead zones, 
  has not been verified. As a first step to test the robustness of the
  RWI in 3D discs, we present customized hydrodynamical
  simulations of 3D protoplanetary discs with an imposed kinematic
  viscosity that is height-dependent. Two types of numerical 
  experiments are performed:
  \begin{inparaenum}[(i)]
  \item non-axisymmetric linear instability of artificial
    density bumps;  
  \item vortex-formation at planet gap edges.%; and 
%  \item vortex-formation in discs with a radial viscosity transition.
  \end{inparaenum}
  
\end{abstract}
